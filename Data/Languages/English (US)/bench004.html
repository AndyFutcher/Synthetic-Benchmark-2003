The Floating Point Unit Benchmark.

 

See Also: The CPU Benchmarks, Operating System Benchmarks, Back to Main.

Other interest: Synthetic Benchmarking Overview, Real-time Benchmarking Overview.

 

The Floating Point Units were at one stage actually known as the co-processors, these days the manufacturers have designed processors where multiple FPU’s work together in the same processor using different pipelines. These processors have designed to handle mainly complicated mathematical calculations, those you would find by the thousands in a computer game graphics engine.

 

FPU’s are best designed to handle double or single precision floating point calculations, which means that these numbers have huge numbers of decimal places and are used to accurately give perspective and precision to a graphical screenshot. Even though the modern day graphics cards take much weight off your main processor, the long and short of it is that the computer processors still has to tell the graphics card what to draw, where, scaling and in some cases fog tables, transform and lighting effects to.

 

Neither is the FPU not used for any other software programs, many business programs and powerful spread-sheet programs make extensive use of the FPU’s mathematical capabilities for there functions and graph rendering.

 

The FPU Benchmarking Types:

 

·          Like with the CPU benchmarking types, the FPU benchmark features two benchmarks, the most important benchmark in the FPU section is the Single precision benchmark, which involves the most used mathematical format for computer games and features a 4 byte value with 10 decimal places. This benchmark will tell you how many single precision calculations SynthMark XP can perform per second on your computer.

 

·          The second benchmarking type is the Integer benchmark, integers are used by your software for smaller calculations and are used extensively loops. It is because of this that integer performance is quite highly regarded in determining the mathematical performance of your applications, software packages, and your operating system.

 

Understanding the FPU Benchmarks:

Each benchmark has its place in the total processing effort of your entire computer. And the FPU is no exception, any graphic, even your Windows GUI uses the FPU’s to some degree and therefore, the FPU benchmarks will always have their place in modern day benchmarking systems.

 

The FPU benchmarks again should be quite a common sight on the SynthMark XP NETMark servers.
