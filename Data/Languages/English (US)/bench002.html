Configuring Benchmarks with SynthMark XP.

 

See Also: Selecting your Benchmarks, Back to Main.

 

Some benchmarks under SynthMark XP can be configured to perform the benchmarks on a specific target device or location. Generally the default settings are good enough for normal benchmarking but in the case of the Web processing benchmark, the target URLs have to manually add.

 

Configuring Hard drive & Cd-Rom benchmarks:

If you have more than one hard drive or cd-rom drive and you want to change the default drive to benchmark, you can click the configure menu option under the benchmarks menu. There is also explorer panel and tool bar button for benchmarking configuration. Clicking any of those links will open a window like the one to the right.

 

You will then see two check boxes with the title ‘hard drive benchmarks’ and ‘cd-rom & dvd-rom benchmarks’, placing a check in these boxes will automatically select all the hard drive or cd-rom benchmarks that SynthMark XP supports.  The drop down box with will contain the list of drives you can select for each benchmark. SynthMark XP checks that there is a enough free space to perform that benchmark before the benchmark will commence.

 

Configuring Web & browsers Benchmarks:

These benchmarks see how quickly SynthMark XP can navigate and download the entire home page of any specific websites you select. You can add target sites into the list by clicking the add button to the right of the list, and the remove button to remove the selected site from the list.

 

Clicking the refresh button will recheck the sites status before beginning the benchmarks.

 

Once you are happy with the current configuration, click the OK button to close the window.
