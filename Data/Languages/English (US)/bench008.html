The Web Processing Benchmark.

 

See Also: Loading & Saving benchmark presets, Back to Main.

 

This benchmark is all about one thing, bandwidth! And not just any bandwidth, turn around time comes into play here too. The objective is to see who computer and internet connection can completely download and visit certain sites before the next guys can. Some of the major differences is this benchmark is configured by your server or by voting.

 

Tweaking your web processing performance:

 

·          If you are the server, choose local websites! J Others can vote in URL’s but you ultimately have the power of choice.

·          Of course your type of connection, ISP performance and local backbone will all have a large effect on this benchmark.

·          Don’t use your connection for anything else when you run this benchmark.

·          NETMark will see to it that even if you are the server, you connection will have a fair chance to compete.

·          Don’t even bother with your internet caching systems, because SynthMark XP will make it re-download the entire web page.

·          Also don’t use abbreviated sites, rather use: http://www.AndyFutcher.com/index.html than just http://www.AndyFutcher.com.

 

When competing with this benchmark, we suggest competing with people who have a similar connection type to you, and even better if they live in your country!

Only then will it be connection orientated, because each country has its own limits when it comes to international site performance.
