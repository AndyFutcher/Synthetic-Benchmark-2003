The SynthMark XP Burn-in Feature.

 

See Also: Synthetic Benchmarking Overview, Back to Main.

 

The SynthMark XP Burn-in wizard is a easy way to loop certain benchmarks continuously, thereby testing your computers endurance and reliability under long period of strain. The biggest advantage of using the SynthMark XP burn in wizard is that you can stop it anytime you need. Rather than other burn in benchmarking systems don’t have to specify how long it must take to complete the benchmark.

 

You can keep the benchmarks running all day if you like. Another advantage is you alt tab SynthMark XP and continue using your computer with reduced performance for your applications, but never the less you can still us it. The Burn in benchmarks are always run in synthetic mode to allow you a bit of freedom while the benchmarks are performed.

 

Why use the SynthMark Burn-in Feature Summary:

 

·          If you want to test the reliability of your computer.

·          You can still use your computer to a certain extent.

·          You can stop it anytime you wish.

 

Please note: You run these benchmarks at your own risk, especially the hard drive burn-in benchmarks. We cannot and will not be held responsible for any data loss that may occur due to hardware weaknesses.

 

How to use the burn in Wizard:

 

1.       Open SynthMark XP, and click the Run drop down menu.

2.       Followed by the Burn-In Wizard option.

3.       You will then see a window like the one shown below open.

4.       Place a check in the check boxes of the benchmarks you would like to run.

5.       Followed by the start button to begin the benchmarks.

6.       Once the benchmarks are running you can stop them anytime with the Stop button.
