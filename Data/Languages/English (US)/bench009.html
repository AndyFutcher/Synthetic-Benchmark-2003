The Network & Internet Benchmarks.

 

See Also: Loading & Saving benchmark presets, Back to Main.

 

The networking and internet benchmarks are really direct data transfer benchmarks. And they work between the server and client at all times, only if you are a client platform can you run this benchmark type, in other words if you have joined another server online and can your run these benchmarks.

 

Although there are some very interesting benchmarks here, we think they are best run in a controlled environment like your home network or business LAN. Internet benchmarks will yield quite low scores because these benchmarks are two way benchmarks (bi-directional). Which means they are not emulating copying data, they are emulating playing a game with heavy use of two way communication performance.

 

Hardware Features play the biggest role in improving the performance of these benchmarks, some of the tweaks are described here:

 

Tweaking the Networking & Internet Benchmarks:

Tweaks for LANS…

1.       Use high quality cables for all your connectivity.

2.       Switches are generally better than HUBs; they often have additional cache memory as well.

3.       Enable full duplex communication on all the network cards if they support it.

4.       Use 100 MBPS cards if your hub supports it.

5.       Try all use the same brand of card, as some manufacturers use multi packet accelerators to speed up communications.

 

Tweaks for Internet…

1.       Make sure your line has no funny noises on it.

2.       Using a higher priority connection of faster uplink always helps.

3.       Try connecting to local servers more often as your connection will be much quicker.

 

The difference network & internet benchmarks:

 

·          The IP ping response benchmark is one where very little data is actually transferred, instead an identification packet is sent and received by each platform, this benchmark is also bi-directional so the packets are not one way packets.

 

·          The data transfer benchmark determines how many kilobytes of data can be reliably transferred between the two computers (server and client) per second. Quite simple really.

 

·          The Compression vs. Raw benchmark is for users who want to see how much of a difference the compression technique makes between the two computers if the random data transferred between the two computers is compressed and decompressed on each side of the transfer.
