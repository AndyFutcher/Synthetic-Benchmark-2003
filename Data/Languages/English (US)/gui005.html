Using the SynthMark XP Explorer Panel.

 

See Also: Using the Keyboard, Differences in the Menus, Back to Main.

 

The explorer panel displays for you exactly what you can do with SynthMark XP as it standing in front of you now. So when you just load SynthMark XP is give you the getting started, the connectivity, services and help & support panels. But then when you load a single benchmark session for example, the panels will change to benchmark options, tools & features, additional tools and the other places panel.

 

So it tried to keep all the common things you would want to do at that time all in one place, making it easier to choose what to next. This is especially useful if you are just a beginner and don’t know where to start. By no means are all the options displayed in these menus, but you should be able to find you way around using just the explorer panels in the beginning.

 

Interacting with the panels:

Pressing your TAB key will cause the little yellow selector shown to the right cycle through the panel headers & the options below it. Then pressing your ENTER or SPACE BAR keys will choose that link and do whatever it said it would do.

 

Moving your mouse over one of the panel header will cause it light up, clicking the panel header will minimize the panel making more space for the panels below it. These panels will stay closed until you open them again (by clicking them) even if you restart SynthMark XP. Moving your mouse over one of the panel options will cause it to be underlined. Clicking it will do whatever the link says.

 

All interaction with the panels is does by a single key press or mouse click. Some panel options can be disabled and when this happens there is no icon and the text is lighter.

 

Users with smaller displays:

If your screen resolution is 800x600 or smaller then you may often have a slider bar running down the right hand side of the panel. Sliding the bar will move the displays up or down to any panels that may be hidden from you view. Using the TAB key will also cause the display to follow the selected object.

 

You can also make the entire panel bigger or smaller by sliding the explorer panels right border to increase your panel views or increase your section viewer.
