Multi-Media benchmarks must be one of the most common benchmarks in the world, with every 3d designer churning out 3d benchmarking systems at the drop of a hat. So we decided to do something different, but that does not count, because we always deicide to do something different, and at the same time made it more interesting.

 

So we did something quite unconventional, we discussed that it always give your games better frames per second (FPS) performance if you disabled sound on your computer games. Then with our experience with DriverMAGIC Pro and all this hardware rot we discovered that with the exact same computer, performance of several games was different depending on what sound cards were installed. So not only do sound cards slow down your games, but some cards slow it down more than others.

 

Wow? Our own little niche benchmarking type. Sound card benchmarking. So we designed SynthMark XP to see how quickly SynthMark XP could upload a tiny high quality wav file into the sound cards play buffer, as determined by the sound cards drivers, some sound cards don’t even have their own onboard play buffers. These buffers are emulated by the drivers into your computers RAM. Slowing them down even more. So we certain had an interesting benchmark here and we have perfected it and launched it with SynthMark XP.

 

The Multi-Media Benchmarks:

 

·          The first benchmark is our unique sound card benchmark, which essentially fills up the play buffers of your sound card to see just how the cards performance and driver design may effect your computer games performance. This benchmark is measured in direct sound buffer injections per second.

 

·          We also have the Windows GUI render, which tests your operating systems ability to manipulated graphics through four sided polygons. This benchmark is scores in polygons per second and is directly related to you windows version, graphics card and graphics drivers installed.

 

·          The video playback benchmark is also related to your graphics card, and determines your computer ability to render a high quality movie file through your graphics card and directdraw if your graphics drivers support it. This benchmark is measured in frames per second (FPS), this indicates a single frame in the movie sequence. A score above 60 means your getting crystal clear playback and your computer is awesome for watching movies.

 

A quick note about sound card benchmarks:

If you are playing music in the background, you may get quite serious jumping noises and audio defects coming through. Generally this should not happen if you are not using your sound card. Also make sure your sound audio volume is quite low with running this benchmark because if the interference comes through, it may be quite loud!

 

A general note about multi-media benchmarks and real-time mode:

Also remember while running multi-media benchmarks that sometimes, the real-time benchmarks can be slower because SynthMark XP is stealing the maximum system resources away from your devices drivers, and therefore compromising its own performance. It all depends on the support of your hardware, and quality of your drivers.
