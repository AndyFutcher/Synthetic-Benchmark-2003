The Operating System Benchmarks.

 

See Also: The CPU Benchmarks, The FPU Benchmarks, Back to Main.

Other interest: Synthetic Benchmarking Overview, Real-time Benchmarking Overview.

 

The benchmarks discussed in this chapter are probably our most controversial. Why? Because everyone’s operating system, weather its 98 or XP is different. There may be different tasking configurations, different kernel versions, different settings and different driver configurations to mention a few.

 

Yes, even driver configurations can make a big difference, for example if you have a printer driver installed with its own management software added and permanently running, we who knows how badly designed it was that it may seriously affect your systems performance. This is why you should always keep your drivers up to date; see our web section on DriverMAGIC Pro.

 

The OS Benchmarking Types:

 

·          The multi-tasking benchmark sees how quickly SynthMark XP can communicate with all the programs running on your system, like DDE (dynamic data exchange) but on a kernel level. Remember when we say all the programs running on your system, we don’t just mean the ones on your start bar, were talking about every single service running on your computer, drivers, TSR’s, anti-virus systems, fax managers and even the start bar program.

 

·          Next in the SynthMark XP operating system benchmark arsenal is CPU vs. Mobo benchmark, even though this is more hardware related, the operating system kernel management does come into play quite heavily here (as you will notice the difference between synthetic and real-time). This benchmark seeks to find out how many CPU operations can be performed per complete memory allocation cycle. So essentially this benchmark answers the question: how many times faster is my CPU over my RAM? Remember that synthetic mode with this one is not hardware related, but rather kernel related.

 

·          And finally comes the Thread distribution benchmark. Now this one is extremely interesting because what SynthMark XP does it via the kernel, command all the other programs to refresh all their objects and elements, as if your system had just switched all the system colors. This causes all the programs & services running on your system to request a large amount of processing requirements for each task, so how well did your kernel manage each program & services task.

 

The Thread Distribution Benchmark:

This benchmark is the height of multi-task benchmarking because it truly sees not only what your kernel is capable of doing when it comes to thread distribution, but also how well is your operating system configured with its services and background programs. If you have big applications running in the background, or larger services and hidden utility windows this score will be seriously decremented.

 

Great theoretical benchmarking is what this section is about, and we believe these three benchmarks are going to cause a stir in the operating systems world!
